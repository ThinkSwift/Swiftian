Pythoneer Community Terms

Last updated: July 3, 2026

Pythoneer lets you publish the games, characters, and tiles you make to a shared Discover feed, where other players — including kids — can play and remix them. Publishing means you agree to these rules.

What you may not publish

  • No hateful content — nothing that attacks or demeans people for who they are (race, religion, nationality, gender, sexual orientation, disability, or anything else).
  • No harassment or bullying — no content that targets, mocks, or threatens another person, in game names, art, or scripts.
  • No sexual content — nothing sexually explicit or suggestive. Pythoneer is played by children.
  • No graphic violence or gore — cartoon game action is fine; realistic cruelty, gore, or glorification of violence is not.
  • No personal information — don't include your own or anyone else's real name, address, phone number, school, or contact details.
  • No impersonation — don't pretend to be another person, creator, or company.
  • No illegal content — nothing that promotes illegal acts, drugs, weapons, or scams, and nothing that infringes someone else's copyright or trademark.

How moderation works

  • Every publish passes an automated content-safety check before it appears in Discover. If the check can't run, the publish is held (fail-closed).
  • Anyone can report a game or creation from its ⋯ menu. Reports are reviewed, and violating content is removed.
  • You can block any creator (Settings ▸ Blocked creators) to stop seeing everything they publish.
  • Accounts that repeatedly or seriously break these rules lose publishing access, and their published content is removed.

Your content

You keep ownership of what you make. By publishing, you give other Pythoneer users permission to play and remix your published games and art inside the app. You can delete your published content at any time, and Settings ▸ Delete account removes your published games, likes, and username.
